HOW COSTING WORKS

Three steps from receipt to job costing.

Connect your CRM and your supplier accounts once. Costing watches for new invoices, suggests where they belong, and lets you confirm with a single keystroke.

01

Invoices arrive on their own.

Invoices can come from anywhere — vendor portals, email receipts, forwarded PDFs, or direct integrations. Costing pulls in PDFs, line items, and totals automatically, so nothing gets re-typed.

02

Costing suggests the matching job.

We rank likely matches using the signals on each invoice and across your job data — names, dates, locations, references, and historical patterns. The best options rise to the top so approvals stay fast.

03

Confirm and Costing posts it.

Pick a line item or create one, hit Enter. The cost is posted to the matched job, the receipt PDF is attached, and your job-costing report stays accurate.

KEYBOARD-DRIVEN MATCHING

Clear an invoice every five seconds.

The whole inbox runs on the keyboard. J/K through the queue, 1–3 to pick a suggested job, Enter to confirm. Hard ones go to Search. Junk goes to Overhead or Ignore. Built for the office manager who'd rather get their Saturday back.

  • Vendor coverage today — Sherwin-Williams (live API sync), Home Depot Pro & Lowe's Pro (email ingest with PDF attachment).
  • Smart suggestions that read the P/O field, the customer name on the receipt, and active job dates. Most invoices match in one click.
  • Pushed straight to your CRM — cost item posted, receipt PDF attached to the job, and job-costing reports that finally add up.
  • Data-integrity guards — spot duplicate invoices, jobs with no cost items yet, and zero-cost line items waiting on a receipt.
